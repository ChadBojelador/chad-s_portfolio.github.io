Sustainable Development Goals

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a collection of 17 global objectives established by the United Nations in 2015 to end poverty, reduce inequality, and protect the planet. The SDGs aim to achieve a greener, healthier, more peaceful, and equal planet by 2030.

What is SDG 16?

SDG 16 aims to promote peaceful and inclusive societies, ensure access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable, and inclusive institutions at all levels. It focuses on reducing violence, ending exploitation and abuse, combating corruption, and fostering transparency and the rule of law. By addressing issues like conflict, injustice, and weak governance, SDG 16 seeks to create stable societies where individuals can thrive and sustainable development can flourish.
